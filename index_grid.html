What are Soybeans?


Soybeans are also called "Meat from the field" because they contain the neccesary fat and proteins and they are a nutritious food. In Japan, soy beans have been cultivated since 1000 B.C.

Britannica Soy Bean

Soybeans are processed into various products. Miso and soy sauce are essential condiments in Japanese culture. Also tofu, natto(fermented soybeans), and edamame are seen on tables everyday. In recent years, soy milk has become popular in the U.S. as well, as an alternative to cow milk.

Condiment

Miso

It is a brown paste type condiment for dipping and marinating. Mixing some into dashi broth makes the famous miso-soup. Cooking soy beans and adding rice or oats with fermentation starter create "Koji", which is then used to make Miso. Different regions make different types of miso. Here is a recipe that uses miso

Recipes that use Miso


Soy Sauce

It is a dark colored liquid type condiment. Soy sauce is used as a dipping sauce for sushi and sashimi, and it can be added into almost any cooking to create more deep, earthy flavore ;) To make soy sauce, soybeans are steamed and then stir fried. The beans are then mixed with wheat and "Koji" and salt are added. The liquid part after fermentation is soy sauce.


Food

Edamame

Salty cooked green beans that go well with beer in summer. Soy beans are cultivated later, but the beans eaten as edamame are cultivated when they are still green. Edamame contain various vitamins, minerals and protein, and can help you recover faster from fatigue and boost liver function.

Tofu

White soft squares that are used in many dishes as a source of protein. Tofu is made from soy milk. When warmed soy milk is mixed with "Nigari", it creates a pudding-like fluffy texture. Freshly made tofu is so delicious,even only with a little salt on top.

Natto

It is fermented soy beans. It has a unique smell and a slimy, sticky,stringy texture. Natto is popular as a breakfast food, putting it on top of white rice with soy sauce. It has many health benefits, containing not only vitamins and minerals, but also probiotics to help your body absorb these nutrients more easily. But....


So many foods that don't look like soy beans are made from soy beans, as if they are sneakingly invading the earth. In fact, it is true that places where soy beans are farmed are drastically increasing, and this causes deforestation because it requires a lot of water to grow. However, much of this soybean crop is not consumed by humans, but instead used in meat, dairy, and egg industries. Around 80% of soy bean production is used to feed live stock.

Are Soy Beans Bad for the Environment?



So what to do?


Eat more soybean products as a protein source and eat less meat!


It sounds contradictory to recommend consuming more soy beans that cause all of those bad things like increasing carbon emissions and strain water sources. However, most soy beans are fed to livestock. If you ate soy beans instead of meat, "agricultural deforestation would decline by as much as 94%" and it is much more sustainable.
